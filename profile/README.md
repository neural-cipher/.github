<h1 align="center">N E U R A L C I P H E R</h1>

<p align="center"><sub>INDEPENDENT RESEARCH LAB · MECHANISTIC INTERPRETABILITY / COMPRESSION / EVALUATION</sub></p>

<p align="center"><strong>Deciphering learned computation.</strong></p>

> Like **Alan Turing** cracked **Enigma**, we decipher **AI black boxes**.

We work at the seam between mechanistic interpretability, compression, and the
strange new physics of learned systems. We publish **circuits, not press releases** —
findings that can be inspected, repeated, and argued with.

---

### What we work on

| | |
|---|---|
| **Interpretability** | *Mechanisms before metaphors.* Tracing circuits, features, and activation paths until a behavior can be named clearly. |
| **Compression** | *What remains when the model shrinks.* Studying the structures that survive simplification, pruning, and distillation. |
| **Evaluation** | *Evidence that can be inspected.* Turning internal traces into figures, notes, and claims others can check. |

### What you'll find here

- **Circuit notes** — small reports on mechanisms that stay stable across probes.
- **Visual atlases** — collections of features, activations, and traces arranged for inspection.
- **Failure reports** — honest accounts of probes that did *not* explain a behavior, and why.

> Circuits, figures, notes.
